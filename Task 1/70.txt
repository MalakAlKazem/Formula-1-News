Had Aston Martin fitted intermediate wet-weather tyres, rather than slick medium dry-weather tyres to Fernando Alonso’s car in Monaco, there’s a very good chance he would have emerged in the lead and gone on to win the Grand Prix. But they didn’t and he crossed the line second for his fifth podium in six races, in a season where the double world champion fulfilled my prediction of scoring at least two rostrums by collecting eight trophies – but didn’t get that win he so craves.
